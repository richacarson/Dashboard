
# Portfolio Positioning — Q1 2026 Geopolitical & Macro Playbook

## Thesis

We are in the most significant geopolitical supply shock since the 1973 Arab Oil Embargo. The Iran War (Day 31) has shut the Strait of Hormuz, taken ~10 million barrels/day of oil offline, destroyed Qatar's LNG export facility, and eliminated 30-35% of global ammonia production. Simultaneously, the Fed is boxed in at 3.50-3.75% with inflation re-accelerating, recession probability has doubled to 30-42%, and the "Great Rotation" is pulling capital out of tech/growth and into energy, industrials, and defensives.

**The core positioning question:** IOWN's current portfolio has significant exposure to both sides of this trade — energy/commodity names that are surging AND tech/crypto names that are under severe pressure. The opportunity is to lean harder into what's working, reduce exposure to what's broken, and position for the second-order effects (food security, defense, infrastructure) that the market hasn't fully priced.

## Key Risks to This Positioning

1. **Rapid ceasefire/Hormuz reopening.** If the war ends suddenly, energy and fertilizer names correct sharply. The long energy/short crypto trade reverses.

2. **Fed pivot.** If recession hits and the Fed cuts aggressively, growth/tech/crypto could rally hard. Our underweight would hurt.

3. **AI spending pullback.** If hyperscalers cut capex due to recession, semiconductor names take another leg down. Currently holding, not adding.

4. **Oil overshoot → demand destruction.** If Brent hits $150+ sustainably, it triggers a global recession deep enough to destroy demand and crash commodity prices.

5. **Gold reversal.** Gold at $4,570 with a 52-wk high of $5,627. A ceasefire + risk-on rotation could trigger sharp gold selloff.

6. **Private credit contagion.** Default rates at 5.5%, Ares/Apollo capping redemptions. If this spreads, financials (SYF) and broader credit markets take a hit.
