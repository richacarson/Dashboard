## ISM Manufacturing Expansion

The ISM Manufacturing PMI has expanded for three consecutive months (January 52.6, February 52.4, March 52.7) following 26 straight months of contraction — the longest streak since 2008-2009. Our research (*ISM Manufacturing Expansion: How to Profit*, April 2026) documents the historical precedent:

- When ISM sustains above 50 for 3+ months after prolonged contraction, cyclical equities outperform the S&P 500 by 800-1,200 basis points over the subsequent 12 months.
- Industrials rank #1 in historical outperformance (+800-1,400 bps), followed by Materials (#2, +600-1,100 bps) and Energy (#3, +400-900 bps).
- This expansion is driven by defense spending acceleration ($924.7B NDAA + $200B supplemental), AI infrastructure buildout, and tariff-driven reshoring ($1.2T in announced capacity).
- **Risk:** Prices Paid at 78.3 — the highest since 2022. If this exceeds 80 for two consecutive months, we would recommend rotating from pure cyclicals to quality industrials.

This signal forms the primary basis for keeping our heavy industrials allocation steady.

## Proposed Dividend Portfolio Changes

### Proposed Exits

**1. Matson (MATX) — Recommend Exit**
*Industry: Marine Shipping | Rationale: Liquidity Constraint*

Matson's average daily volume of ~300,000 shares at ~$160 produces daily dollar volume of roughly $48 million. For Model 7A at around $385 million AUM, a 4% allocation ($15.4M) would represent over 31% of daily volume.

- Screener score of 65 (HOLD) — among the weakest in the dividend book
- Estimated tax impact: Mostly long term gain

**2. Agilent Technologies (A) — Recommend Exit**
*Industry: Life Sciences Tools & Services | Rationale: Sector Rotation & Tax Loss Harvest*

Carson's thesis on Agilent has not changed. It remains a high-quality business. However, the macro environment has shifted materially since we added this position in January, and the current healthcare overweight (16% vs 9% target) should be corrected in favor of energy exposure which is currently only at 4% weight.

- Healthcare allocation currently at 16%, well above our 9% target
- Energy allocation at only 4% — critically underweight given the Iran War backdrop
- Estimated tax impact: short term (some clients already sold out in TLH)

### Proposed Additions

**1. Nutrien (NTR) — Recommend Addition**
*Industry: Agricultural Inputs | Thesis: Fertilizer Crisis Beneficiary & Long-Term Compounder*

Our original research identified the Iran War's destruction of Qatar's Ras Laffan facility as eliminating 30-35% of global ammonia production — the most significant fertilizer supply disruption since WWII. Rebuilding takes 3-5 years, making this structural, not temporary. Global urea prices are up ~50%.

Nutrien is the world's largest potash producer with integrated nitrogen, phosphate, and retail distribution. Critically, it operates from Saskatchewan — zero exposure to Middle Eastern or Russian supply chain risk.

- Materials sector ranks #2 in ISM post-contraction outperformance (+600-1,100 bps)

**2. Coterra Energy (CTRA) — Recommend Addition**
*Industry: Oil & Gas E&P | Thesis: Dual-Basin Energy Exposure*

CTRA provides both oil (Permian) and natural gas (Marcellus) exposure, addressing a gap — VLO is a downstream refiner, CTRA is upstream production. Different parts of the value chain that perform differently across conditions.

The gas side matters especially now. Qatar's LNG destruction removed 20% of global supply, and European gas prices are trading at ~19x U.S. Henry Hub. Coterra's Marcellus operations are positioned for this structural export demand that didn't exist six months ago.

- Energy sector ranks #3 in ISM post-contraction outperformance (+400-900 bps)

### Proposed Additions

**1. Freeport-McMoRan (FCX) — Recommend Addition**
*Thesis: Structural Copper Demand*

A single hyperscale AI data center consumes 15,000-50,000 tons of copper. Our optical interconnect research confirms net copper demand still grows — data centers are being built faster than copper intensity per facility is declining. Add EVs (3-4x more copper than ICE vehicles) and grid modernization, and supply deficits of 400,000+ tonnes are forecast for 2026. New copper deposits take 10-15 years from discovery to production. Freeport operates Grasberg (world's largest copper/gold mine), Peru, and Arizona.

- Score: 82 | PEG FWD: 0.43 | D/E: 34%

**2. Credo Technology (CRDO) — Recommend Addition**
*Thesis: Data Center Interconnect Silicon*

As GPU clusters scale to hundreds of thousands of chips, the interconnect fabric between them becomes the performance bottleneck — not the GPUs themselves. Credo's SerDes and active electrical cable chips sit at that exact chokepoint. This is a different supply chain node than photonic components — Credo makes the high-speed silicon that converts signals for transmission.

- Score: 82 | PEG: 0.30 — strongest valuation-to-growth of any proposed addition | ROE: 27.5%

**3. Vistra Corp (VST) — Recommend Addition**
*Thesis: Nuclear Power & AI Data Center Electricity Demand*

U.S. power demand is forecast to grow 50% by 2040 driven by AI data centers, reshoring, and EVs. Nuclear provides 24/7 baseload with zero emissions — exactly what hyperscalers are contracting for. Vistra's nuclear and gas generation assets have direct exposure to wholesale power prices and capacity market premiums. Pairs with EIX: regulated utility alongside independent producer.

- Score: 75 | ROE: 17.66%

**4. Marvell Technology (MRVL) — Recommend Addition**
*Thesis: Custom AI Silicon & Photonic Fabric*

Data center is 74% of revenue, growing 46.5% YoY. Marvell's 1.6T PAM4 DSP chips are the industry standard for converting signals between electrical and optical domains. The Celestial AI partnership adds photonic fabric — using light to move data between chips. This positions MRVL alongside CRDO in our optical interconnect thesis: CRDO makes the high-speed connectivity silicon, MRVL provides custom AI silicon plus the photonic conversion layer. Fills the 25th slot.

- Score: 86 — highest among proposed additions | PEG: 1.2 | ROE: 22% | Rev: +42% YoY

## Points for Committee Discussion

**1. Technology Weight in Growth (40%)**
XLK scores 1.5 (Weak) on our technical matrix. Our framework does suggest underweighting. However, growth represents approximately 30% of aggressive client portfolios, meaning total portfolio technology exposure is approximately 12% — well below the S&P 500's ~30%. Three of five proposed growth additions (COHR, CRDO, MRVL) are thesis-driven from original research. Carson recommends maintaining this allocation, especially since tech is what gives a growth portfolio "umph" in an up-market.

**2. Growth Financials Exposure (18%)**
XLF scores 1.5 (Weak). Four of six financial names (COIN, HOOD, HUT, MARA) are crypto-adjacent, and crypto has already entered its own confirmed bear market and found support. SUPV scores 46 — the lowest in the entire portfolio. Carson is not recommending exiting SUPV this quarter due to his own technical analysis, but is watching it for a possible Q3 sell. Carson is also monitoring the crypto market for our financials exposure:

- If crypto has no recovery by mid-May → bring COIN/HUT/MARA weight reduction proposal to committee for Q3 rebalance

**3. Industrials Overweight vs. Technical Signal**
Carson's proposed industrials overweight to 18% is a fundamental conviction override of the technical score (2.5). XLI is above the 200 DMA but below the 50. Carson believes the ISM manufacturing expansion signal justifies forward-looking positioning, but this represents a deliberate deviation from a strict technical-only approach.

**4. Prices Paid Monitoring**
ISM Prices Paid at 78.3 is the primary risk to the cyclical overweight thesis. If this exceeds 80 for two consecutive months, Carson would consider rotating weight from pure cyclicals (STLD, PCAR) to quality industrials with pricing power (CAT, FAST) in Q3.

**5. Taxes for GFI**
GFI has been a top performer in the Growth Portfolio, and the consequence of selling now versus waiting until July is something for the committee to consider. If the committee agrees that we should NOT sell GFI in our current rebalance, then the proposal is to buy the copper stock replacement anyway and hold 26 stocks in the growth portfolio until we can let go of GFI.
