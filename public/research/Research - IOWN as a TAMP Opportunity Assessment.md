
# IOWN as a TAMP — Opportunity Assessment

## Thesis

The TAMP (Turnkey Asset Management Platform) market is a ~$3 trillion ecosystem growing at 12.6% CAGR, with advisor adoption climbing from ~10% to ~45% over the past decade. There is a clear whitespace in the faith-driven TAMP market — no dominant player offers turnkey faith-based model portfolios to other advisors. IOWN/Paradiem, managing ~$516M with established Inspire screening methodology and a proprietary "Return on Intention" framework, is positioned to fill this gap. The question is whether the infrastructure investment and operational complexity is worth the revenue opportunity, or whether IOWN's resources are better spent growing its direct AUM.

## What Is a TAMP?

A TAMP provides outsourced investment management, technology, and operational support to financial advisors. Instead of building their own models, advisors plug into the TAMP's platform and focus on client relationships.

**Core services a TAMP provides:**
- Model portfolios (equity, fixed income, multi-asset)
- Portfolio construction and rebalancing technology
- Trading and execution
- Performance reporting
- Compliance support
- Billing and fee calculation
- Tax-loss harvesting

**Two types of TAMPs:**
1. **Product TAMPs** (AssetMark, SEI) — Compete with proprietary investment strategies
2. **Platform TAMPs** (Orion, InvestCloud) — Compete on technology, open-architecture

## The Faith-Driven TAMP Opportunity

### Current Landscape
- **850 faith-based funds and ETFs globally**, ~$100B combined AUM
- **Inspire Investing** — world's largest faith-based ETF provider (IOWN already uses their screening)
- **Thrivent** — offers faith-based managed portfolios through its TAMP, built with multiple Christian asset managers
- **AssetMark** — has published on faith-based investing integration but doesn't specialize in it

### The Whitespace
**No dominant faith-based TAMP exists.** There are faith-based funds (Inspire, Eventide, GuideStone) and TAMPs that can accommodate faith screens (AssetMark), but no platform that is **built from the ground up** for faith-driven advisors with:
- Pre-screened faith-based model portfolios
- Proprietary frameworks like "Return on Intention" (Innovation, Inspiration, Infrastructure)
- Integration with Inspire screening methodology
- Marketing and client materials oriented toward faith-driven families
- Understanding of 100+ year family legacy planning

### Addressable Market
- ~14,000 RIAs in the US
- Estimated 10-15% identify as faith-based or values-aligned (~1,400-2,100 firms)
- Average RIA AUM: ~$500M
- If IOWN captured 5% of faith-based advisor AUM: **~$35B-50B** opportunity
- Even 1% penetration at 50 bps = **$17M-25M/year**

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Recurring revenue:** TAMP fees are sticky, recurring, and scale with AUM
- **Mission alignment:** Extends IOWN's faith-driven investing mission to more families
- **Competitive moat:** First-mover advantage in faith-based TAMP space
- **Leverage existing expertise:** IOWN already runs the models — TAMP just distributes them
- **Scalable:** Adding $1B in TAMP AUM doesn't require proportional staff growth
- **Brand building:** Establishes Paradiem/IOWN as the thought leader in faith-driven investing

### Cons
- **Distraction risk:** Building TAMP infrastructure takes focus from direct client service and AUM growth
- **Technology cost:** Platform build or licensing is $200K-$1M+ upfront
- **Compliance complexity:** TAMP operations add regulatory layers (ADV amendments, model disclosure)
- **Support burden:** Advisor clients need training, support, and handholding
- **Track record requirement:** Advisors want 3+ year track records before adopting models
- **Competition:** Envestnet or AssetMark could launch faith-based sleeves and compete with scale

## Risks

1. **Build vs. buy cost overrun.** Technology platform development is notorious for budget overruns. White-labeling (GeoWealth, Orion) reduces risk but also reduces margin.
2. **Slow adoption.** Faith-based advisors may prefer existing relationships with Inspire or Eventide over a new TAMP from a smaller RIA.
3. **Track record gap.** Without 3+ years of verified TAMP model performance, institutional advisors won't bite.
4. **Regulatory scrutiny.** TAMP operations invite more SEC scrutiny around conflicts of interest, fee layering, and best execution.
5. **Market timing.** Launching during a potential bear market could result in poor early track record.
